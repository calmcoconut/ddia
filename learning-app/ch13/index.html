Interleaved Scenario Challenge

Mixes all topics · Builds discrimination · Forces transfer · ~10 min

Each scenario requires you to combine knowledge from multiple sections of Chapter 13. This deliberate mixing forces your brain to select the right framework — the hallmark of expert-level understanding.

Scenario 1 of 4

🎫 Global Concert Ticketing System

You are designing a high-throughput ticketing platform for major concerts. The system needs to:

  • Prevent the same seat from being sold twice (strict uniqueness check)
  • Keep regional query search indexes updated with event and seat details
  • Track ticket sales in real-time for promoter dashboards
  • Handle sudden massive load spikes (millions of concurrent users booking tickets) without system outages
Scenario 2 of 4

🚚 IoT Logistics Fleet Tracker

A logistics company tracks 100,000 delivery trucks in real-time. The trucks stream GPS coordinates, engine temperature, and cargo status to a central system:

  • Regional dispatchers need up-to-date truck locations on live maps
  • Data analysts run nightly routes optimization jobs
  • Automated maintenance systems trigger alerts if a truck's engine overheats
Scenario 3 of 4

🏦 Multi-Currency Digital Wallet

You are developing a global digital wallet where users hold balances in multiple currencies and can transfer funds instantly to other users. You want to scale the system globally using a multi-leader database setup across three datacenters (US, EU, Asia) to ensure low latency.

  • Transfers should be fast and available even during temporary inter-region fiber cuts
  • Account balance states must remain correct and consistent without disappearing money
Scenario 4 of 4

⚖️ Trade-Off Tribunal

You are the Chief Architect at a financial analytics firm. The product manager wants to build a new feature: a real-time portfolio value tracker. The engineering team is divided into two camps:

  • Camp A: Advocates for an ACID-compliant, strictly serializable relational database to guarantee that all calculations are 100% accurate at all times.
  • Camp B: Advocates for a fully unbundled, log-based, eventually consistent dataflow system using Kafka and Flink to achieve maximum scalability and low-latency updates.
