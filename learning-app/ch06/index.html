The Scenario

You are designing the storage system for a global social media platform where users can post status updates and comment on other posts. You expect:

  • Millions of daily active users distributed across North America, Europe, and Asia.
  • A high read-to-write ratio (99% reads, 1% writes).

Interleaved Scenario Challenge

Each scenario requires you to combine knowledge from multiple sections of Chapter 6. This deliberate mixing forces your brain to select the right framework — the hallmark of expert-level understanding.

Scenario 1 of 4

🗺️ Collaborative Global Travel Planner

A startup is building a collaborative travel planning app:

  • Users can create shared itineraries (list of destinations, hotels, flights)
  • Friends in different countries (US, Germany, Singapore) can edit the same itinerary concurrently
  • The app must support offline editing when users are on planes or have poor connectivity, and sync when they reconnect
  • Replicas are deployed in US-East, EU-Central, and Asia-Southeast
Scenario 2 of 4

📈 Real-Time Stock Portfolio Dashboard

A financial platform needs to display real-time stock portfolio valuations:

  • Portfolio value depends on stock prices (updating constantly) and user transactions (buy/sell)
  • Users check dashboards frequently and demand that when they execute a buy transaction, the portfolio valuation update is visible immediately
  • The platform uses a sharded database for scale, with each shard replicated across multiple regions for low read latency
Scenario 3 of 4

🛒 Flash Sale Inventory System

A major retailer is hosting a high-traffic flash sale:

  • Product inventory is extremely limited (e.g., 100 designer handbags)
  • Hundreds of thousands of concurrent users are trying to buy the same handbag
  • The database is replicated across multiple regions for scale
