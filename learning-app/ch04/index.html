Interleaved Scenario Challenge

Mixes all topics · Builds discrimination · Forces transfer · ~10 min

Each scenario requires you to combine knowledge from multiple sections of Chapter 4. This deliberate mixing forces your brain to select the right framework — the hallmark of expert-level understanding.

Scenario 1 of 4

🚕 Ride-Hailing Matchmaker

You are designing the data layer for a real-time ride-hailing app. The system must:

  • Process 20,000 updates/sec of driver GPS coordinates.
  • Handle matching queries looking for active drivers within 2km of a customer.
  • Store historical trip data for fraud analysis and pricing models (billions of trips).
Scenario 2 of 4

🏦 High-Volume Financial Ledger

A retail bank is building a ledger system. Every transaction must be appended immediately. The system must support:

  • High-frequency write-only transactions (appends).
  • Strict requirements for crash recovery and audit trails (no data lost, ever).
  • Account balance lookups (fast queries by account ID).
Scenario 3 of 4

🛒 E-Commerce Analytics Pipeline

You are the data architect at an online retailer. The marketing department wants to run ad-hoc queries analyzing sales trends (e.g., "What was the total revenue for blue shirts sold to customers aged 25-35 in Ohio?"). The transaction database is a MySQL (InnoDB) database.

  • InnoDB stores data row-oriented (B-tree).
  • Marketing queries need to scan millions of records.
  • We need to isolate these reports from production customers.
Scenario 4 of 4

🔀 Trade-Off Tribunal

You must make a ruling on each trade-off. There is no "right answer" — only well-reasoned arguments.
