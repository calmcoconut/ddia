          "trade_off_analysis": "Normalized schemas store a single canonical copy of each entity and link them via identifier pointers (foreign keys). This minimizes write overhead and guarantees consistency across records but penalizes read latency due to the CPU and disk seek overhead of executing multi-way joins. Denormalization duplicates human-meaningful data within records to allow single-seek reads (high performance), but incurs higher write latency, requires additional storage, and risks structural inconsistencies unless managed by transaction-safe databases or external application coordination."
